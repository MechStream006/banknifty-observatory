# Observations

A structured log of qualitative market observations — patterns noticed, anomalies encountered,
and contextual notes from reviewing collected data.

**Important:** Observations are **not evidence.** They are inputs to hypothesis formation.
An observation that looks like a pattern is the start of a question, not an answer.
All quantitative claims must go through the hypothesis → evidence pipeline.

**Rules:**
- Date every observation.
- Note the market session, regime context (trend/range/volatile), and data quality status.
- If an observation suggests a hypothesis, link the observation to the hypothesis once registered.
- Do not act on observations — they inform hypothesis design.

---

## Observation entry format

```
### OBS-NNN — Title
**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Session type:** [Expiry day | Pre-expiry | Post-expiry | Normal]
**Regime context:** [Trending up | Trending down | Range-bound | High volatility | Unknown]
**Data quality:** [Clean | Gaps noted | See integrity report YYYY-MM-DD]

**Observation:**
What was seen. Quantitative if possible; qualitative otherwise. No interpretation.

**Possible hypothesis:**
If this observation could motivate a hypothesis, sketch it here.
Link to H-NNN if already registered.

**Action:**
[Register hypothesis | Monitor further | No action | Discard — explained]
```
